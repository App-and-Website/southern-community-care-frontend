Our Advocacy Work: A Voice for Change, A Shield for Rights

At Southern Community Care, advocacy is not an optional extra, it is a fundamental part of our mission. We believe in speaking up, both for the individuals we serve and for the systemic changes needed to create a more just and supportive community for all. Our advocacy is two-fold: we fight for the rights of the person in front of us today, and we work to improve the system for everyone tomorrow.

Individual Advocacy: Protecting Your Rights and Your Voice

We act as a steadfast ally for our clients and their families, ensuring your voice is heard and your rights are respected in all situations. Our individual advocacy includes:
  • Upholding Your Rights: We ensure you are always treated with dignity and respect. We empower you by clearly explaining your rights regarding privacy, consent, and choice in your care.
  • Navigating Complex Systems: When you face challenges with funding bodies (like the NDIA, My Aged Care, or DVA), service providers, or housing, we step in. We help you understand complex letters, lodge complaints, and communicate effectively to resolve issues.
  • Attending Appointments: With your permission, our advocates can accompany you to medical, legal, or financial appointments to help you ask the right questions, understand the information, and ensure your wishes are clearly communicated.
  • Resolving Disputes: If you are unhappy with a service or decision, we can support you through formal complaint processes, acting as a mediator to ensure a fair outcome.

Our goal in individual advocacy is to empower you to self-advocate, while knowing we are right beside you with unwavering support when you need it.

Systemic Advocacy: Driving Change for a Better Future

We use the collective voice and experience of our organization to address the root causes of inequality and inefficiency within the community care sector. Our systemic advocacy work involves:
  • Policy Submission and Government Engagement: We formally submit responses to government inquiries and proposed policy changes. We provide evidence-based recommendations to simplify access to services, increase funding levels, and improve the quality and safety standards across aged care, disability, and veteran support.
  • Sector Leadership and Collaboration: We actively participate in peak bodies, working groups, and industry forums. In these spaces, we share the real-world challenges faced by our clients and staff, pushing for collaborative solutions that benefit the entire community.
  • Raising Public Awareness: We run campaigns and use our platforms to shine a light on critical but often overlooked issues, such as elder abuse, the gaps in rural service delivery, and the specific needs of younger people in residential aged care. We educate the public to build a groundswell of support for change.
  • Championing Workforce Development: We advocate for better pay, conditions, and professional development for community care workers. A supported, skilled workforce is essential for delivering the high-quality care our community deserves.

Our goal in systemic advocacy is to fix the system itself, creating a future where navigating care is less of a battle and where policies are inherently more compassionate, equitable, and effective.

Our Advocacy in Action: Real-World Impact
  • This is not just rhetorical. Our advocacy has led to:
  • Successfully appealing unfair NDIS plan decisions to secure crucial funding for participants.
  • Providing formal testimony that contributed to a state government review on aged care transport access.
  • Partnering with other organizations to launch a local campaign combating social isolation among seniors.

We are committed to being a catalyst for change, both in the life of one person and in the landscape of an entire system.

If you’re facing a challenge, experiencing an issue, or believe something needs to change, we’re here to listen. Our team is ready to stand beside you, understand your concerns, and take meaningful action to help create positive outcomes for you and your community.
